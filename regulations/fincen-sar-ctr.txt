FinCEN — Suspicious Activity Reports and Currency Transaction Reports (United States)

Source: Synthetic summary of the U.S. Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) and FinCEN regulations.
For training/demo use only; not legal advice and not a verbatim official text.

1. Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs)
A U.S. financial institution must file a Currency Transaction Report for each cash
transaction in currency of more than USD 10,000 conducted by, or on behalf of, one
person in a single business day. Multiple cash transactions are aggregated and treated
as a single transaction when the institution knows they total more than USD 10,000 and
are by or on behalf of the same person. The CTR is threshold-based and required even
without any suspicion.

2. Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)
A bank must file a Suspicious Activity Report when it detects a known or suspected
violation of federal law or a suspicious transaction related to money laundering or
other illegal activity. General SAR dollar thresholds for banks are: USD 5,000 or more
when a suspect can be identified; USD 25,000 or more regardless of whether a suspect can
be identified. A SAR must generally be filed within 30 calendar days of initial
detection of facts that constitute a basis for filing. SAR filings — and their very
existence — are confidential; "tipping off" the subject is prohibited.

3. Structuring under the BSA
Structuring is the act of breaking up currency transactions to evade the CTR filing
requirement — for example, making several cash deposits of USD 9,000 instead of one
deposit of USD 18,000. Structuring is a federal crime under 31 U.S.C. 5324 whether or
not the underlying funds are illicit. When a bank detects structuring it must file a
SAR describing the pattern, even though no single transaction exceeded USD 10,000.

4. Red flags
- Cash transactions consistently just under USD 10,000.
- A customer who asks about reporting thresholds or how to avoid a CTR.
- Deposits split across multiple branches, ATMs, or accounts in a short period.
- Sudden, unexplained activity inconsistent with the customer's known profile.
