Know Your Customer (KYC), Customer Due Diligence (CDD) and Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

Source: Synthetic summary of common AML/KYC principles drawn from FATF Recommendations
and typical national CDD rules. For training/demo use only; not legal advice.

1. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
CDD is the process of identifying a customer and verifying their identity using reliable,
independent source documents, data, or information. Reporting entities must:
- Identify and verify the customer's identity at onboarding.
- Identify and verify the beneficial owner — the natural person(s) who ultimately own or
  control the customer (commonly a 25% or greater ownership threshold for legal entities).
- Understand the intended nature and purpose of the business relationship.
- Conduct ongoing monitoring of the relationship and scrutinise transactions to ensure
  they are consistent with the customer's profile and source of funds.

2. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)
EDD applies to higher-risk customers and situations and requires additional measures,
such as obtaining senior management approval, establishing the source of wealth and
source of funds, and conducting enhanced ongoing monitoring. Higher-risk factors include
Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs), customers in high-risk jurisdictions, complex or
unusually large transactions, and ownership structures designed to obscure control.

3. Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
A PEP is an individual entrusted with a prominent public function. PEP status, including
family members and close associates, raises the risk of bribery and corruption, so PEP
relationships require EDD and senior management sign-off.

4. Sanctions and watchlist screening
Customers and counterparties must be screened against applicable sanctions lists (for
example OFAC, UN, and national lists). A confirmed match generally requires blocking or
rejecting the transaction and reporting as required by the relevant regime.

5. Simplified Due Diligence (SDD)
Where money laundering and terrorist financing risk is assessed as low, a reduced set of
CDD measures may be applied — but SDD is never a complete exemption from monitoring.
